May's all time favorite movies

My best 3 movies

The Shawshank Redemption

1994 American drama film written and directed by Frank Darabont. The film tells the story of banker Andy, who is sentenced to life in Shawshank prison for the murders of his wife and her lover, despite his claims of innocence. The movie shows that Andy's ability to maintain his hope and to fight for his freedom, even in the face of adversity. Another central theme is the importance of friendship and human connection, as exemplified by the bond between Andy and Red, which becomes a source of strength and support for both men throughout their hard time in prison.


3 Idiots

"3 Idiots" is the story of three engineering students: Farhan, Raju, and Rancho as they navigate through college life in India. Through their adventures, the trio learns valuable life lessons and discovers the true meaning of success and happiness. The movie delivers a powerful message about the importance of prioritizing personal happiness over societal expectations. It highlights the flaws in the education system and the pressures faced by students to excel academically at the expense of their well-being. Rancho's unconventional approach to learning challenges the traditional education system, leading to humorous and heartfelt moments.


Dead Poets Society

"Carpe diem.
Seize the days, boys.
Make your lives extraordinary."
Dead Poets Society is a film set in a conservative boys' prep school in 1959. English teacher John inspires his students to embrace poetry, individuality, and seizing the day. Through the formation of a secret club, the Dead Poets Society, they explore their passions and challenge the school's rigid norms. Tragically, conflicts arise when one student's pursuit of his dreams clashes with his family's expectations, leading to a poignant exploration of conformity, rebellion, and the power of mentorship.
